## What the Best Sites Always Include
- Clear headline stating the service and location (e.g. "Bathroom Installation Skegness")
- List of specific services (not just "bathrooms" — break it down: suites, wet rooms, tiling, underfloor heating)
- Phone number prominently displayed, ideally both mobile and landline
- Years of experience or trading history
- Mention of qualified/insured tradesmen
- Gallery or portfolio of completed work
- Local area references (Skegness, Lincolnshire coast, surrounding villages)

## What Trust Signals Appear Most
- Years trading (20+ years is common in this area)
- "Fully insured" and "fully guaranteed"
- In-house team (not subcontracted)
- Multiple contact methods
- Local knowledge / community presence

## What CTAs Convert in This Sector
- "Call now" with prominent phone number (primary)
- "Get a free quote" form (secondary)
- "Book a free consultation" / "Free 3D plan"
- Direct phone number in the hero section is essential — tradespeople's customers prefer calling

## Visual Tone
- Professional but approachable — not corporate, not overly casual
- Clean whites, soft greys, with an accent colour (navy, teal, or warm gold)
- Real bathroom photography showing finished installations
- Lifestyle-aspirational for higher-end work, practical/clean for standard bathroom fitting

## Recommended Section Structure
1. **Hero** — Business name, location, one-line value proposition, phone number, CTA button
2. **Services** — 3-4 core services with brief descriptions
3. **Why Choose Us** — Trust signals: years trading, qualifications, guarantees, local
4. **Process** — 3 simple steps: enquire, design, install
5. **Social Proof** — Reviews/testimonials, star ratings
6. **Gallery/Portfolio** — 2-3 images of completed bathrooms
7. **Contact/Enquiry** — Form + phone number + address
8. **Footer** — Business details, phone, area served
